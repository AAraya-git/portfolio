📊 BI Management System

A full relational data system built in Google Sheets for an apparel design studio — modeling designs, materials, orders, and inventory the way a real database would, with automation replacing manual tracking end to end.

The problem

The studio was running on scattered spreadsheets and Notion pages, mixing designs, garment sizes, and colors into one confusing "products" list. There was no reliable way to see real margins per item, inventory quietly went out of sync with actual stock, and inconsistent naming between records (like "Blanco" vs. "Blanco hueso") was already causing order mix-ups before the system existed.

What I built

  • A plain-language home menu — quick-access links in everyday terms ("Register a New Order," not raw sheet names), so non-technical staff can use the system daily without training
  • A real relational data model — separated the business into independent catalogs (Designs and Materials/blanks), joined through a SKU table where each Design + Material combination auto-generates its own SKU, cost, and margin
  • Automated ID generation — client, supplier, order, and material IDs are all generated automatically from what's typed in, eliminating manual entry errors
  • BOM-driven inventory — a bill-of-materials sheet automatically deducts raw materials when a batch is printed and adds to finished-goods inventory, keeping "printing" and "selling" as distinct, auditable events
  • Built-in data validation — purchase, production, and order entries are checked against master tables in real time, flagging typos before they become bad data
  • Live Kanban order tracking — orders automatically sort into New / In Progress / Finished / Delivered based on their production and delivery status
  • Volume-based supplier pricing — purchase costs are suggested automatically based on tiered supplier rates and quantity purchased
  • Flat export tables feeding a live Looker Studio dashboard for real-time sales, margin, and inventory analytics
  • A documented data dictionary mapping every sheet's primary/foreign keys — laying the groundwork for a future migration to a proper SQL or Airtable database

Impact

Migrated the studio's existing client list and inventory records from Notion into the new system — cleanly flagging the handful of records that didn't have an exact match instead of silently creating bad data. The system now tracks 70 clients, 230 orders (221 active/completed), 313 individual order line items, and 73 sellable SKUs across 5 designs, with real-time computed margins averaging 70–78% per item — replacing what used to be manual spreadsheet reconciliation with a system that updates itself.

Working with AI

I designed the full data model, business logic, and system architecture myself — decisions like separating Designs from Materials, structuring the BOM to keep printing and selling as distinct events, and the validation rules all came from understanding how the business actually works. I used Claude to speed up execution: writing formulas faster, catching edge cases, and building this portfolio site. It's a tool that accelerates implementation, not a substitute for the thinking behind it.
